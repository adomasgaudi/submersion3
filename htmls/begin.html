TWO CHOICES

A lot of things could influence you language learning. However, I don't want to bother people with life philosophy advice if that's not what they're looking for. So to keep it organised, I've separated the material into two groups.

There are a lot of things that interest me that are related to language learning. For example, productivity, neuroscience, creative writing, philosphy, ect.... Though I think I'll indulge myself here and there with some off-topic posts I would still like to make a distinction between topics that are more directly related to language and less directly related.

"More directly related" does not necessarily mean "making a greater impact". It simply means that it is more obviously related, not more strongly. For example, if you have some urgent job-related issues that are just burning like a house fire, I'm sure that it would probably be more beneficial for you to deal with those problems and then think about language learning. Equally, something like your sleep, nutrition, motivation, habits, your reasons for studying and your life phisolophy, all of those things could be much more influencial than, say, something as directly related as a specific exercise, but nonetheless, I would like to separate them into a different category

Everything relating to life choices that end up giving us more time with our target language or making us better learners or making us more interested or focused, those things will be under the "life" category
