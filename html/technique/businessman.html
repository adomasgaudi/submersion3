The business man

The business man and the native are the two most common groups. Striving for relative proficiency in the language but within reasonable bounds.

The business man approach is the lower level, probably to around 5-8 thousand words (B2 level). At this level, most people will be largely ignorant of their grammar mistakes and will not be able to perceive any accent error above their level.

It is important to distinguish the Immigrant at a b2 level from a businessman at a b2 level, as an immigrant will be able to perceive grammar mistakes and will be much better at recognising accent errors, though still not able to speak as well or have a large vocabulary. The business man at a B2 level is at his peak. An will not desire higher proficiency.

Advantages

The business man is not as worried about his accent, thus he can do more reading and more writing and speaking, ignoring the fact that it will hider his accent.

focusing on reading and speaking to perfect understanding will be powerfull beneficial tools.
